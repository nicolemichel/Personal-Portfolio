How it all took off

Early 2018

takes Google Quiz

The story of my coding journey is a bit of a bumpy ride, so be safe and strap in.

It starts just after high school.
I’m 18 with no idea what I want to do for a career so I decide to continue working my retail job at The Source and take some time to figure it out.

A few years go by and I realise I have a love for technology but definitely not in an I.T. type of way. Being new to the idea I had no clue what other tech jobs were possible so I turned to the internet for an answer.

Yup, you read that right.
I took a Google quiz to figure out what I wanted to do for a career. Crazy right? But when I read the result of “Programmer” and the little blurb that explained what that was, it felt like a perfect fit. I mean I like problem solving, I’m creative, a good team player, and adaptable.

That brings us to step two.

I'm now 21 with a good idea of what I want to do, unsure of what school I want to go to and feeling like I’m behind in life with the need to make up for lost time.

Bring in my sister, Kelsey (31) looking for a career change, also feeling the need to make up for lost time - she finds a college an hour away that halves the typical time needed for a degree. A college that deals only in business, technology and healthcare (looking like the universe is doing me a favour and piecing things together for me at this point). Anyways, she had a tour lined up and I decided to tag along to see what they were about.

I ended up sitting in on a class, loving it and signing up to start just three short weeks later.

2018-2020

attends triOS College

We’re now at step three.

It’s April 16, 2018 I’m starting my first class with absolutely no idea what I’m doing, and behind. That’s right, a bump in the road right from the start.

Since this college lets you start at the beginning of any course new students come and go all the time, and because the professor didn’t know I would be starting so soon he began the course a week early (he made up for it with some one on one time). Skip forward a bit and it’s April 27, 2018 just 9 classes later and it’s exam time. The professor is standing at the front of the class and telling us that we have 4 hours to code a page that looks the same as what he has displayed on the whiteboard (to give you an idea it was basically a page that looks similar to a four square box you find painted on school grounds). He also tells us that if we need help we can ask - but marks will be deducted anytime he has to come over (this is fair as it’s an exam and we should know what we are doing - regardless panic ensues).

I was just starting to get the hang of things and hate asking for help already, so to know points will be deducted encourages the stubbornness.

Time seemed to be moving slower and slower in this exam, the class was slowly emptying out as students finish and leave, and my stress level heightens as I struggle with getting some boxes to sit left and others right. Eventually I cave in and ask for help, the professor comes over, sits down and explains things and while my exam looked horrible it was laid out in the fashion he wished and I completed the class with a 79%.

I’m grateful for that exam to this day because it challenged me (if the course was too easy I probably would have gotten bored, dropped out and been back at step one) and I quickly learned that it’s okay to ask for help when needed. The rest of the course time felt like it flew by, exams and project due dates passed, and next thing you know it was time for my internship.

Which brings us to step four, my internship.

This was another tiny bump in the road, because I was travelling to London everyday for class I was hoping to find something in Sarnia so I didn’t have to travel and could focus on the work I would be doing. Luckily the career assistance triOS provides was able to find me a placement at tmrrw inc just in time.

From October 2019 to January 2020 I was doing front end work on a few different sites - from fixing layout issues to doing page animations and at the end of my 400 hours my course was completed and I graduated with honours (3.9 GPA/86% average).

SheCodes online courses

That brings us to step five, post graduation.

Since my time at triOS was my only coding experience I was majorly lacking confidence in my coding abilities and terrified of my student debt so here we will take a quick U-Turn because I ran back into my comfort zone (sales). I discovered Brookstone Windows and Doors on Indeed, applied and started a field marketing job. I won awards for highest appointment rate, highest validation rate, rep of the quarter (won an XBox Series X), and I was also promoted to Team Lead in less than a year.

Now bring in the biggest bump, COVID-19. With lockdowns happening on and off I decided to refresh my brain, expand my knowledge and boost my coding confidence with SheCodes.

Now we are pulling up to a stop sign, step six, this current moment.

I’m still not overly confident in my coding abilities but I'm aware I can’t stay in my comfort zone forever and am willing to take the risks.

Are you willing to take them with me?
